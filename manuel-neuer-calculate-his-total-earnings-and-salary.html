Manuel Neuer: His Soccer Journey

Manuel Peter Neuer, born on March 27th, 1986 in Gelsenkirchen, (then) West Germany, is a professional goalkeeper for the German side Bayern Munich and a member of the German National team. His career began in 2004.

Curious about Manuel Neuer’s wealth as of early 2017? As per authoritative sources, it’s estimated that Neuer’s net worth is a substantial $40 million, along with a current yearly salary of $9 million.

Interested in soccer since the age of two, Manuel joined Schalke 04 in 1991, going through its youth system before making his first-team debut in the 2006-2007 season, eventually cementing his place as a starting goalkeeper. Over time, he became one of the leaders at Schalke and was named captain for the 2010-2011 season. However, he outgrew Schalke’s ambitions and decided not to extend his contract. Instead, he made a transfer worth €22 million to join Bayern Munich, making him the second most expensive goalkeeper at that time, after Gianluigi Buffon.

Neuer has continually improved his form, making crucial saves in the 2012 UEFA Champions campaign, including key penalty saves. Despite some setbacks, Neuer’s time at Bayern Munich has been marked by success, winning several titles alongside the team. His accomplishments extend to the German National team, where he played a major role in their 2014 FIFA World Cup victory. Manuel has also had personal success, winning various awards and being recognized for his talent and contributions.

In his personal life, Manuel was in a relationship with Kathrin Glitch from 2009 until 2014. According to media reports, he is currently single. Neuer is also known for his philanthropy, particularly for starting the Manuel Neuer Kids Foundation, and has earned money for charity through his participation in a celebrity edition of “Wer wird Millionär?”, the German version of the popular TV game show “Who Wants To Be A Millionaire”.
