On January 2, 1968, Lisa Marie Presley was born in Memphis, Tennessee, in the United States. She is multitalented personality, an American singer, songwriter, television actress, and philanthropist. She is also the daughter of Priscilla Presley, a successful entrepreneur, and the legendary singer Elvis Presley.

She released her debut musical CD, "To Whom It May Concern," in 2003, having been born into wealth. Except for the song "The Road Between," she penned all the lyrics.

Her first album received a gold certification and peaked at No. 5 on the Billboard 200 album chart because of her distinctive voice and uncompromising music. She held a show in the UK to promote the same.

What Happened To Lisa Marie Presley?

Lisa Marie Presley had gone through a lot of horrible pasts. Benjamin Keough, Presley's son with her ex-husband Danny Keough, passed away in July 2020 at the age of 27 due to a self-inflicted gunshot wound, according to TMZ.

To put it mildly, Lisa has had an intriguing love life. From 1988 through 1994, the singer-songwriter was first married to music producer Danny Keough, with whom she had two children (late Benjamin Keough and actress Riley Keough).

After divorcing Keough, she married Michael Jackson, the world's biggest celebrity. After two years of marriage, the duo filed for divorce in 1996, citing their irreconcilable differences.

She later briefly engaged to actor Nicolas Cage, whom she had met at a party in 2000, before breaking it off with guitarist John Oszajca. They got married two years later and remained married until their divorce in 2004.
