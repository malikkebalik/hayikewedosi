Life Story of Lil Skies

On August 4, 1998, in Chambersburg, Pennsylvania, Lil Skies was born. His father, Michael Burton Jr., formerly known as Dark Skies, introduced him to music when he was just four years old.

The family moved to Waynesboro, Pennsylvania, for the third grade. When Skies was eleven years old, his father was hurt in a chemical explosion at a Rust-Oleum business nearby Williamsport, Maryland.

He graduated from Waynesboro Area Senior High School in 2016 and was awarded a certificate. After that, he temporarily enrolled in the Shippensburg University of Pennsylvania before dropping out to focus solely on his musical career.

Career

In October 2016, Lil Skies had the opportunity to support Fetty Wap at Shippensburg University, which aided in the growth of his internet fan following. He had established himself at that point on SoundCloud and YouTube.

In 2016, he released “Da Sauce,” his second video, which became an instant hit and attracted one million views. In the same year, Skies released his debut mixtape, “Good Grades, Bad Habits 2.”

He later released his debut mixtape, “Alone,” in January 2017 after quitting college. He started working on his debut album, which would be released on the independent label “All We Got.”

In July 2017, he dropped two songs: “Off the Goop” with Sprite Lee and “Red Roses” with Landon Cube. “Nowadays,” a different Skies song that was released in December 2017, fared better on the American rap charts. The Billboard Hot 100 chart saw the first appearances of “Red Roses” and “Nowadays,” which reached their peaks at positions 55 and 69, respectively.

The next year, he went on tour with Lil Uzi Vert, and Atlantic Records had already worked out a deal with him financially before the release of his mixtape “Life of a Dark Rose.”

The tape quickly gained popularity after it was made widely available. The album debuted on the Billboard 200 at number 23 and received a mixed bag of reviews from critics.

On May 4, 2018, he released his “Lust” music video, and the RIAA certified it as platinum. The song’s peak on the Billboard Hot 100 was at position 87. On March 1, he made his music CD “Shelby” available.
