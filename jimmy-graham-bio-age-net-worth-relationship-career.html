Jimmy Graham is a renowned American football player who currently plays tight end (TE) for the Chicago Bears of the National Football League (NFL). Born and raised in Greensboro, North Carolina, the 6’7″ football tight end has also played for the New Orleans Saints, Seattle Seahawks, and Green Bay Packers.

What is the Net Worth of Jimmy Graham? Salary, Earnings

Jimmy Graham, an All-Pro tight end, has a net worth of $15 million as of October 2020. His profession was the primary source of his wealth.

According to ESPN’s Field Yates, the newly inked tight end will receive a $6 million signing bonus and a $3 million base compensation from the Chicago Bears.

Where was Jimmy Graham born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Jimmy Graham was born on November 24, 1986, in Goldsboro, North Carolina. At the age of 11, his mother placed him in a group where he was physically abused by other children.

A year later, he was adopted by a high school student counselor. He improved his academics as a sophomore at Community Christian in Wilson, North Carolina, and went on to become a basketball superstar.

Graham received a basketball scholarship to the University of Miami after excelling as a sophomore and senior in high school at Charis Prep in Wilson, North Carolina.

NFL star Jimmy Graham is unmarried as of 2020. The All-Pro tight end is currently remaining close-lipped about his relationship and companion.

How did Jimmy Graham start his Professional Career?

Week 9 of the 2010 NFL season, Jimmy Graham made his debut against Carolina. Receiving a 19-yard pass from quarterback Drew Brees, he scores his first NFL touchdown of his career.

In addition, the 33-year-old became the first Saints tight end to surpass 1,000 receiving yards in a single season.

From 2015 to 2017, Jimmy played tight end for the Seattle Seahawks for three seasons, establishing personal records for receptions, receiving yards, and receiving touchdowns.

He inked a three-year, $30 million contract with the Green Bay Packers on March 16, 2018. In 2018, he started 12 games and recorded 55 catches for 636 yards and two touchdowns on 55 targets.

On March 26, 2020, the tight end inked a two-year, $16 million contract with the Chicago Bulls. In his first game of the season against the Detroit Lions, he caught three receptions for 25 yards and a touchdown. The Bears won the contest by a tally of 27-23.

The 33-year-old tight end seems to care more about his professional career than about courting. He resides in Miami, Florida at present.
