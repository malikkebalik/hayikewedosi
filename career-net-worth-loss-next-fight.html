Emanuel Navarrete | Career

Like every athlete, Emanuel Navarrete commenced early in his career at just 17.

As an amateur, Navarrete has fought a total of 14 fights, which concludes with 13 wins, 1 loss, and 0 draws. 

Right in between that tenure, Navarrete had his longest winning streak of 8 fights.

After his debut in February 2012, Navarrete fought against Misael Ramirez and took the victory over via TKO in the first round. 

Back then, he became the first person to have ever stopped Ramirez. Altogether, he was successful in setting the perfect debut of his career.

The same year, Navarrete partook in 7 fights against fighters like Ivan Donovan, Ismael Garnica, and Ramon Pena.

Following it, Navarrete fought against Daniel Argueta on July 26, 2012, which is marked as the first defeat in his career.

As his sixth professional bout, he gained a loss via UD in a valiant effort. Therewith, he went to fight over Ramon Pena and Antonio Gutierrez, which was a total win.

Additionally, he took down Emmanuel Dominguez on August 8, 2014, via TKO during the fourth round. 

Likewise, he stopped Enrique Bernache and Marco Gonzalez from further wins while becoming the first to beat Alberto Luna.

Advancing on, Navarrete again claimed the victory over Ricardo Roman on November 28, 2015. 

Well, Navarrete took the win via KO in the third round of the bout. Similarly, he took over Osvaldo Castro on March 5, 2016. Well, he defeated him via TKO in the fifth round.

Emanuel Navarrete vs. Isaac Dogboe

Initially, Navarrete faced Issac Dogboe on December 8, 2018, at the Hulu Theater in New York City.

Back then, he took him down by unanimous decision (UD) for the WBO junior featherweight title. 

Alongside the fight, Navarrete entered with a 20-fight win streak. Following it, the duo again had their rematch on May 11, 2019, at the Tucson Convention Center.

Right then, the rematch played out as the same as the former match, with long straight shots and solid hooks to Dogboe.

As a matter of fact, Navarrete dominated almost every round in his favor; however, the bout still took its length to the 12th round.

Throughout the round, Dogboe was clearly hurt; however, he took it to the 12th round until Dogboe’s trainer finally threw in the towel. 

Altogether, the bout in Tucson, Arizona, ended with a technical knockout (TKO) victory in his first title defense.

“It was a great fight, and Emanuel Navarrete fought like a true Mexican warrior. Champions are supposed to keep going under any circumstance, but I just couldn’t get the victory. The best man won tonight.”
-Issac Dogboe
